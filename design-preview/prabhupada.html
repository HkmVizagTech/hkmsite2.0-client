A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada

1 September 1896 — 14 November 1977

Founder–Acharya of ISKCON

His Divine Grace A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada is the Founder-Acharya of the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, founded in 1966 in New York City.

"Chant Hare Krishna and be happy."

— Srila Prabhupada

As a 70-year-old, Srila Prabhupada travelled to New York with the sole purpose of fulfilling his guru's dream of spreading the message of Krishna Consciousness. Starting from a tiny office, he went on to ignite a worldwide phenomenon.

Born on 1st September 1896 in Calcutta, he first met his spiritual master Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Goswami in 1922. In 1944, he launched 'Back to Godhead', an English fortnightly magazine now published in forty languages.

Over a span of twelve years, he authored more than 80 volumes of authoritative English translations and commentaries on India's most important religious classics — including the Bhagavad-Gita As It Is and the multi-volume Srimad-Bhagavatam.

Life Timeline

1896

Birth in Calcutta

Born as Abhay Charan De on 1st September 1896 in Calcutta, India.

1922

First Meeting

Met his spiritual master Srila Bhaktisiddhanta Saraswati Goswami who instructed him to spread Krishna consciousness in the English language.

1944

Back to Godhead

Started the magazine "Back to Godhead" single-handedly — writing, printing, and distributing it on his own.

1959

Took Sannyasa

Renounced material life and accepted the sannyasa order to dedicate himself fully to spiritual mission.

1965

Voyage to America

At the age of 70, embarked on a 40-day cargo voyage to America with only 40 rupees, suffering two heart attacks along the way.

1966

Founded ISKCON

Established the International Society for Krishna Consciousness in New York — what began in a small storefront grew into a global movement.

1977

Mahasamadhi

Left this world on 14th November 1977 in Vrindavan, leaving behind over 100 temples, millions of books, and a worldwide movement.

Core Teachings

Three pillars of devotional life as taught by Srila Prabhupada

01

Chant Hare Krishna

Chanting the mahamantra is the easiest and most effective spiritual practice for this age of Kali.

02

Read the Bhagavad-Gita

The Gita As It Is presents Krishna's eternal words without any speculative interpretation.

03

Serve Krishna with Love

Bhakti yoga — devotional service performed with love and faith — is the ultimate goal of life.
