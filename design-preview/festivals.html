List of Major Festivals in India

Home/Festivals

India is known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant festivals celebrated throughout the year. This 2026 festival calendar brings you a complete list of the most significant traditional and spiritual events observed across India. From Sankranti to Janmashtami, from Navratri to Diwali — find all major dates, tithis, and festive highlights in one place.

August

August 2026

Aug 9, 2026

Balaram Purnima

The appearance day of Lord Balarama, Krishna's elder brother. Devotees observe fasting and special celebrations.
Aug 26, 2026

Janmashtami

The most auspicious appearance day of Lord Sri Krishna. Celebrated with abhishekam, kirtan and midnight aarti.
Aug 27, 2026

Nandotsav

Festival celebrated the day after Janmashtami, marking Krishna's birth celebration by Nanda Maharaj.
Aug 30, 2026

Vyasa Puja

The appearance day of Srila Prabhupada, founder-acharya of ISKCON.
September

September 2026

Sep 11, 2026

Radha Ashtami

The appearance day of Srimati Radharani, eternal consort of Lord Krishna.
Sep 23, 2026

Vamana Jayanti

The appearance day of Lord Vamana, the dwarf incarnation of Lord Vishnu.
Sep 30, 2026

Indira Ekadashi

Sacred fasting day in the month of Ashwina. Performing this vrata bestows blessings.
Sep 5, 2026

Lalita Sasthi

The appearance day of Lalita Sakhi, the topmost gopi friend of Srimati Radharani.

Later in 2026

Dussehra

Celebrating Lord Rama's victory over Ravana — triumph of good over evil.
Oct 27, 2026

Kartik Deepotsav 2026

The month-long festival of lights — offering lamps to Lord Damodara.

Govardhana Puja

Annakoot festival celebrating Krishna's lifting of Govardhana Hill.

Gita Jayanti

The sacred day when Lord Krishna spoke the Bhagavad Gita on Kurukshetra.
